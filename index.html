Introduction

Black holes are among the most mysterious objects in the universe. They are invisible regions of space where gravity is so powerful that nothing not even light can escape. Scientists study black holes to better understand gravity, space, time, and the evolution of galaxies.

What is a Black Hole?

A black hole is a region in space where matter has been compressed into an incredibly small area. This creates such strong gravity that everything crossing its boundary becomes trapped forever.

Characteristics

  • Invisible to naked eye
  • Extremely strong gravity
  • Formed from massive stars
  • Bend space and time
  • Can grow by absorbing matter

History of Black Holes

1783 - John Michell proposed the idea of "dark stars.

1915 - Albert Einstein introduced the Theory of General Relativity.

1916 - Karl Schwarzschild discovered the mathematical solution describing black holes.

1974 - Stephen Hawking introduced Hawking Radiation.

2019 - Scientists captured the first image of a black hole.
